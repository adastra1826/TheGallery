TAVOLE DI ABETE · CURVE A 42° · 250 METRI AL GIRO

INNO ALLE TAVOLE

The city outside is fog and tram-bells. In here it is thirty degrees of pine resin and a hundred and eighty beats a minute.

The boards do not sing — they rumble, low, like weather kept indoors. Tubulars at nine atmospheres hiss over the grain. You do not steer on a velodrome; you agree with it. Lean, and the turn carries you like a wave that never breaks.

Winter belongs to the road men, they say. Let them keep the mud. The boards belong to us.

«CHI NON OSA IN CURVA, NON VINCE IN RETTILINEO.»
