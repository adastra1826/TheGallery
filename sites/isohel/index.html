Marginalia IN THE BUREAU'S HAND · UNCHANGED SINCE 1951

On the design of legends

The bureau's founding beliefs were never written as a manifesto. They were written as a legend — because that is what a legend is: everything the mapmaker is prepared to swear to.

  • The legend is a contract. Every mark on the sheet is sworn in here, or it does not appear at all.

    CL. 01

  • Precision is a courtesy. A summit published at 2 871 metres is a promise made to whoever climbs up to check.

    CL. 02

  • Names in italic flow; names in capitals stand still. Water moves, and the type must move with it.

    CL. 03

  • The scale bar is the only sentence on a map that cannot exaggerate. Design everything else to deserve its company.

    CL. 04
Plate III — Services THREE PRACTICES · ONE DRAFTING ROOM

What the bureau draws

Every commission passes over the same drafting table, under the same lamp, past the same standards. The subjects differ; the discipline does not.

Trail & Summit Maps

Walked before drawn. Our surveyors time every ascent, count every switchback, and only mark water that runs in both seasons. Sheets carry slope-shaded climbs and honest, unromantic distances.

Ski Atlases

Winter turns the same mountain into a different country. Atlas plates tint every aspect steeper than 30 degrees, chart lift lines and skin tracks separately, and print the avalanche bulletin's grid squares in the margin.

Invented Worlds

For novelists and game studios: ranges that never were, surveyed as though they had always been. We will contour your dragon's roost at 1:50 000 and argue with you about where the rivers go. (We are right about where the rivers go.)
