LEGERDEMAIN

An academy of sleight-of-hand, one flight above Café Miramar.
The coffee is downstairs. The truth is negotiable up here.

Auditions Thursdays at dusk · Admission by invitation, or by nerve

Über die Ethik der Täuschung

“We lie with our hands precisely so that we may lie about nothing else. The audience signs the contract at the door — deceive me beautifully, and tell me nothing false about how it felt — and for one hour everyone in the room is honest about being fooled. Nowhere else in Vienna is that arrangement available.”

— Rudolf Marek, „Der Uhrmacher“ · founding address, 11 November 1911

Erster Akt · The Standing Rehearsal

The deck rehearses nightly.

Four flourishes from the house repertoire, performed by fifty-two employees who are never paid and never tired. Call one.

Zweiter Akt · A Demonstration, Free of Charge

Das kleine Orakel

Twenty-one cards. Choose one in the privacy of your skull — don’t touch it, don’t name it, don’t tell the café below. I will ask three small questions; answer by touching a pile. Then I will tell you what you are thinking.

Frage IFrage IIFrage III

The table is being prepared.

The method is honest arithmetic; the feeling is not. That difference is the entire curriculum.

Die Auflösung

Three answers, one centre.

Twenty-one cards in three piles of seven. Each time you answer, your pile is gathered between the other two, and the deal by rows spreads its cards across the middle of every new pile. Your card is squeezed toward the centre of the pack: after one answer it is among seven, after two among three, after three it stands exactly eleventh — the heart of twenty-one. No sleight, no memory, no confederate under the table.

We spend the first week of Semester I teaching this arithmetic, and the remaining fifteen teaching you to make it feel like the room tilted. The first lesson is free. You have just had it.

Vierter Akt · Das Kollegium

The faculty keep their stage names.

Court cards age better than faces. Turn one over.

Der Uhrmacher

Rudolf Marek · Rektor

Studied watchmaking in Prague until the day he understood that a pass is only an escapement for the hands. Has not been seen shuffling since 1904 — decks simply arrive in the order he prefers. Teaches the Volte, and the ethics seminar, in that order, on purpose.

König ♠ · Rektor

Madame Coeur

Odile Brandt · Professorin für Nahsicht

Performed forty consecutive nights at the Ronacher without ever using the stage — table to table, sleeve to sleeve. Insists the double lift is a matter of breathing, not fingers. Grades kindly; deceives more kindly still.

Dame ♥ · Nahsicht

Der Kassier

Jakob Fenyö · Dozent, late of Budapest

Kept the books at a card room on Andrássy út until the mathematics became more interesting than the money. Can faro a borrowed deck while wearing gloves. Teaches shuffle theory, and the tram schedule of quiet exits.
