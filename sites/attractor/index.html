Wall text · Gallery 2

On Authorship

When this museum acquired Reef Logic in March 2024, no object changed hands. The work arrived as 212 lines of JavaScript and a signature. We did not hang a painting; we agreed to keep a promise — that whenever a visitor stands in front of the work, the museum will run it.

This is the quiet scandal of generative art. The artist does not make the image. The artist makes the conditions for the image: a field of forces, a rule for sticking, a pair of frequencies that will never quite agree. Then the artist steps back, the seed is drawn, and the work happens — the way weather happens. What hangs on our walls is not the artist's hand but the artist's intent, executed fresh each time by a machine that cannot repeat itself.

Visitors sometimes ask which rendering is the real one — which state we keep in the vault. The answer is none of them. The vault holds procedures. The states belong to you: the exact body of smoke you watched form this afternoon existed once, for you, and was gone when you scrolled past. A museum of objects preserves what the artist saw. A museum of procedures preserves what the artist meant, and lets the seeing be yours.

We conserve no pigment here. We conserve rules, and rules do not crack, fade, or yellow. In two hundred years, given electricity and arithmetic, this collection will still be exactly — and never — what it is today.

— Dr. Ines Kowald, Curator of Computational Works
Reprinted from the exhibition catalogue, spring 2026

The Museum

ATTRACTOR is a museum without a building. Admission is free and perpetual; the collection executes on demand, in any browser, at any hour. There is no vault — only this page.

The Works

Six algorithms in roughly 1,900 lines of hand-written JavaScript. No image files were used anywhere on this floor: every mark you saw was computed in front of you, from a seed drawn from your device's entropy.

The Labels

Wall lettering is set in Archivo; the catalogue voice is Newsreader. Both are self-hosted. This page makes no network requests beyond its own fonts and code.
