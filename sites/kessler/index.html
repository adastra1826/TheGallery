KW/DOCTRINE · ON STEWARDSHIP

The sky does not clean itself

// Orbit is a commons. There is exactly one, and everyone’s grandchildren live downstream of it.

At orbital speed, mass is beside the point. A fleck of paint at seven kilometres per second arrives with the energy of a rifle round; a ten-centimetre fragment arrives like a truck. There are no small objects up there — only small warnings.

Altitude is destiny. Below about 600 kilometres, the thin last breath of atmosphere drags everything down on a schedule of years, and the sky wipes itself clean. At 800 kilometres a discarded bolt outlives the engineer who torqued it. At 1,000, it outlives her language. The census above is not a snapshot of a problem; it is a map of where we chose to be careless.

In 1978, Donald Kessler and Burton Cour-Palais worked out the arithmetic of crowding: collisions make fragments, and fragments make collisions. Past a certain density the cascade feeds itself and stops caring whether anyone keeps launching. The ledger above is not a history. It is a rate.

Stewardship is unglamorous work. Vent the tanks. Short the batteries. Clear the lane on schedule, not eventually. Track everything, and publish what you track, because a conjunction warning withheld is a collision co-authored. Lighthouses were never glamorous either. The watch is kept anyway.

KW/T-1
Passivate everything

Vent every tank, short every battery, spin down every wheel at end of mission. A dead satellite should be inert, not patient.

KW/T-2
Five years, then down

Clear low orbit within five years of retirement. Twenty-five was a courtesy to a smaller sky.

KW/T-3
Track it or don’t fly it

An object you cannot see is a collision you cannot dodge. Radar cross-section is a design requirement.

KW/T-4
Share the catalog

Conjunction data is safety-of-life information, not a trade secret and not a bargaining chip.

KW/T-5
Leave GEO clean

Boost derelicts to the graveyard ring 300 kilometres above the belt while the last kilograms of fuel remain. The belt is finite real estate on an infinite lease.
