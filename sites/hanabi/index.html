間を打つ

The guild's craft

We measure a show by its silences.

A shell climbs for nine seconds. In that hush ten thousand people on the river bank stop talking, stop fanning themselves, stop breathing — and the dark water goes still enough to hold the whole sky. Then the break. Since 1873 the Kawaragi family has packed every star by hand: pine charcoal, rice paste, patience. The colour lasts two seconds. The silence is ours to shape; the colour is merely how we end it.

When a shell breaks perfectly round, the old crowds shouted the maker's name. Listen for ours: Tamaya!

Four breaks, one river

Every shell below is live. Choose a caliber, press fire, and watch the sky above this page — the mortar stands on our barge, mid-river. Climb time is true to gravity; larger shells climb longer.

  • 菊

    No. 07

    Kiku Chrysanthemum

    The strictest test of a maker. Every star carries a tail, so the break must be a perfect sphere — one lazy afternoon at the packing bench and the whole town sees your ellipse. Gold into amber, edge sharp as a compass line.

    tailed stars · sharp sphere · burn 1.8 s

  • 牡丹

    No. 12

    Botan Peony

    The chrysanthemum's quieter sister: same sphere, no tails. Pure points of colour that swell and vanish, pink to rose to a violet ember. Our peony stars are rolled thirty-eight layers deep and cut the night like pinpricks.

    bare stars · no trails · burn 1.5 s

  • 柳

    No. 19

    Yanagi Willow

    Stars cut heavy with charcoal burn long and fall far. The break droops into gold-green branches that reach down for the river and very nearly touch their own reflection — the only shell we aim at the water, not the sky.

    drooping streamers · long fall · burn 3.8 s

  • 冠

    No. 23

    Kamuro Crown

    Named for a child's bobbed haircut. Slow-burning gold that refuses to leave: the crown hangs, glitters, sinks almost to the far bank's lanterns before it lets go. Fourth-generation Isao spent eleven years on this star. It shows.

    persistent glitter · crown 4.5 s · signature shell

五代の系譜

Lineage 五代の系譜

Five generations

  1. I

    Kawaragi Genzō 川原木源蔵

    master 1873 – 1901

    A saltpeter merchant's third son who fired his first shell off a rice barge to mark the end of a cholera summer. Founded the workshop; set the house rule that still stands — every star passes through a family hand.

  2. II

    Kawaragi Shinpachi 川原木新八

    master 1901 – 1934

    Brought magnesium white to the valley and burned off both eyebrows proving it. After the 1923 earthquake he fired three silent white shells over the river every night for a month, for the dead of Tokyo.

  3. III

    Kawaragi Tomiko 川原木富子

    master 1934 – 1968

    The guild's first woman master. Kept the license alive through the wartime powder ban by making signal flares, then spent the 1950s perfecting the peony core — thirty-eight layers, rolled wet, dried on the roof in June wind.

  4. IV

    Kawaragi Isao 川原木勲

    master 1968 – 2002

    Eleven years on a single star formula: the slow gold that became our kamuro crown. Refused every offer to mass-produce it. "A crown you can buy in a box," he said, "is a hat."

  5. V

    Kawaragi Ren 川原木蓮

    master 2002 – present

    Choreographs firing sequences on a laptop and still packs stars at the same bench as Genzō. Under Ren the guild fires eleven towns a summer. Her rule for the finale: "Fill the sky once. Then stop while they're still looking up."
