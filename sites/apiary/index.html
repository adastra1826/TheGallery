The Co-op

Forty-one members, one smoker, and no landlord among the bees.

We started in 2019 with two hives beside the water tower at 143 Delancey, because the deck was empty and the lindens two blocks over were not. The building said yes before anyone thought to ask what a swarm looks like from the eighth-floor window.

Seven seasons later the co-op runs seven Langstroth hives across three rooftops. We raise our own queens rather than ordering them by mail, split strong colonies instead of buying packages, and jar every harvest by rooftop and season — never blended, never heated past hive temperature. Every drop came from a roof you can see from the fire escape.

Dues cover woodenware, sugar for the lean months, and the honey share. Arguments about frame spacing are settled by whoever is holding the smoker.

The Harvest

We lift the frame. It does the rest.

Two extractions a year, in the stairwell landing we are not technically allowed to call a honey house. Every jar is single-rooftop and single-season — strained once through a coarse sieve so the pollen stays in, never heated past the 95°F the hive already keeps.

Field Science

Direction is an angle. Distance is a duration.

A forager who finds nectar worth telling the room about comes home and dances it. On the vertical comb, straight up stands in for the sun: she runs her waggle line at the angle her flight made with the sun’s bearing, and the length of the waggle — about one second per kilometer — says how far. Karl von Frisch decoded it in 1945. Our bees never stopped broadcasting.

Live from the dance floor of Hive H01 — pick a forage run our bees actually dance:

  • She repeats the circuit up to a hundred times per dance.
  • Followers read it in the dark, by touch and by air current.
  • Livelier waggle, richer nectar — enthusiasm is part of the message.

Membership

No experience required. Veils provided.

Dues are $85 a season and cover gear, sugar, and your share of the harvest — members jar first, before a single label goes on sale. Inspections run Sundays at 9 a.m., April through October, rain postponed to the same afternoon. You’ll be handed the smoker on your second Sunday and trusted with a frame by your fourth.

We take twelve new members a year, drawn from the waitlist in January. Sponsorship of Hive H08 — the 2027 expansion split, Odessa line — is open now.

Applications open January 12 at the winter meeting — 143 Delancey St, stair C, tenth floor. Bring gloves; we have veils.
