INSIDE THE ISSUE · PP. 4–23 · ONE NIGHT APIECE

CLOCKED IN

Five people keep Gullhaven running between dusk and dawn. We stayed up with each of them and typed what we saw. Everything below came off the duplicator slightly crooked — rest a hand on a page to pull it into register.

21:30

9:30 p.m. — THE PRINTER

Otto Fenn, 61

Fenn Duplicator Works · 44 Cannery Rd.

“People say riso is unpredictable. The machine is fine. It’s the people who are unpredictable.”

Otto prints church bulletins by day and everything else by night — setlists, seed catalogs, this zine. At 21:30 he switches off the shop radio, cranks the GR3770, and lets the pink drum drift, because perfect registration is for cowards. He proofed this page. He says the misalignment is intentional. We believe him.

11:58 p.m. — THE NURSE

Deion Marsh, RN

St. Brigid’s Emergency · Ward Street

“Four a.m. is the honest hour. Nobody comes in at four a.m. for something small.”

Deion clocks in two minutes before midnight — never earlier, a superstition he refuses to explain. Twelve beds, one vending machine that eats dollar coins, and a shift that runs on drip coffee and triage math. He keeps a tally of sunrise discharges on the whiteboard: 1,204 and counting.

3:40 a.m. — THE BAKER

Marisol Quade

Ovenbird Bakery · Delancey Row

“The city thinks bread appears at seven. Bread appears at 3:40 — it just isn’t ready to talk to anyone yet.”

Marisol’s alarm is three alarm clocks, staggered, on the far side of the room. By four the ovens are singing and the radio is tuned to Lena. Sixty baguettes, ninety rolls, and one experimental rye she describes as “still in beta.” The first loaf is always burned on purpose. For luck.

FROM THE KITCHEN TABLE

DEAR READER,

PULP is assembled at a kitchen table and printed at Otto’s while the kettle recovers. This issue kept us up — on purpose, for once. We followed five Gullhaveners through one full night each and wrote down only what we actually saw.

No composites, no “sources say.” Just the small hours, typed up.

Go to bed. After you read it.
— E.
