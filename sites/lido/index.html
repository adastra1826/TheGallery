— 1,2 m · la parte bassa —

Novant'anni,
quarantacinque estati

The basin was poured in the spring of 1936 — fifty metres of white concrete on the headland above the old harbour, finished in forty-one days by crews under municipal architect Attilio Bonfante. It opened on 21 June with a brass band, a bishop, and a hundred and forty children who refused to get out at closing.

The pumps failed in October 1978, and the town, short of money, let the water go. For forty-six years the empty basin baked in the sun — skateboarded on, argued about, never unloved. Divers still climbed the fence at dusk just to sit on the ten-metre board.

Restoration began in 2023, tile by original tile. Lido Solare reopened on 21 June 2025 — eighty-nine years to the day. Same basin. Same board. Same water temperature nobody agrees on.
