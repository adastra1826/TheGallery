Vinterprotokollen · house rules of the dark season

Fifty days without a sunrise. We dress you for every one of them.

Tromsø sits at 69.6° north, four hundred kilometres above the Arctic Circle. From 27 November to 15 January the sun never clears the horizon. We have outfitted travel through that dark since 1911 — first for hunters and telegraph crews, now for people who want to cross a plateau behind their own dogs and sleep warm at minus twenty-four.

We are not a tour company. We are outfitters: the route is planned to the hour, the kit is weighed to the gram, and the rest is between you and the weather. Groups stay small. Phones stay in the pulk.

Ekspedisjoner · winter 2026–27 · departures from Tromsø

Three routes into the dark

Every departure is guided, provisioned and fully kitted from our own stores. Prices are per traveller, from Tromsø, everything included except your courage and your camera.

Mørketid — polar-night photography

Five nights on the west shore of Kvaløya, where the noon light holds a two-hour blue that photographs like held breath. Days are for sea stacks, drift ice and the patient geometry of the shoreline; nights are for the aurora watch from a heated shore hut at Ersfjordbotn. Tripods and −40° clothing issued. Patience assumed.

Vidda — dog-sled plateau crossing

Two hundred and twelve kilometres from Camp Tamok over the Dividalen high plateau, each traveller driving their own team of five Alaskan huskies. You feed them before you feed yourself; by day three they will forgive your braking. Nights are spent in expedition tents at minus twenty and below — warm, because the kit is correct and the routine is kept.

Isfjord — ice-fjord kayak

Four days of paddling among drift ice in the Lyngen fjords, under alps that rise eighteen hundred metres straight from the tide line. Sea eagles by day; by night, the aurora over Strupen watched from the pier sauna, eighty metres from your bunk. Drysuits issued and mandatory. Cold water is honest — it tells you everything at once.
