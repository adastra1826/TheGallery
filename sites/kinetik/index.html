Air, made visible.

We build hanging mobiles that truly balance — aluminium leaves weighed to a tenth of a gram, hung on steel wire, tuned until they move the way weather does. The sculpture beside you is not a film. It is being computed right now: your cursor is wind, your scrolling is weight.

The craft

One gram times one centimetre.

A mobile is an argument about weight, settled rod by rod. Mass times distance on the left must answer mass times distance on the right — 120 grams close to the pivot can be held by sixty, hung twice as far away. Slide the brass weight until the beam agrees. That feeling, when the beam goes quiet — that is the entire craft. We repeat it thirty-one times in an average mobile, always starting from the smallest leaf.

Studio

Balanced from the bottom up.

KINETIK is Mara Voss, two assistants, and a two-room workshop on the Keizerstraat — a former sailmaker's loft with a ceiling built for hanging things. Mara spent nine years fairing hulls before she bent her first mobile wire; the obsession with fair curves and honest weight came along.

  1. 1

    CUT

    Leaves are sheared from 1.5 mm annealed aluminium and filed until the edge disappears from the thumb. Then two coats, front and back: Calder red, cobalt, sun, iron black.

  2. 2

    WEIGH

    Every leaf is logged to a tenth of a gram in the studio ledger. Paint counts — two coats of red add 1.1 grams to a palm-sized disc, and the ledger knows it.

  3. 3

    BALANCE

    Assembly starts at the lowest leaf and climbs. Each new beam slides across a knife edge until the moments agree, then the loop is closed with one turn of a round-nose plier.

  4. 4

    HANG

    On site we tune to the room's own weather — vents, doorways, stair drafts — bending the top wire in whole degrees until the sculpture and the air agree.

Commission

A mobile for your air.

We take eight commissions a year. It starts with a visit: we measure your drafts, count footfall, and watch daylight cross the room for an afternoon. Ten to fourteen weeks later a sculpture arrives that belongs to that exact air — balanced in the studio, tuned in place, guaranteed for twenty-five years of motion.
