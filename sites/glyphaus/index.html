Specimen story

Built to be read from a moving S-Bahn.

Kolosse began as a lettering job for a Hamburg crane operators’ union — twelve letters, four metres tall, painted on corrugated steel. The brief said visible in fog. The union dissolved before the invoice cleared; the letters stayed. Version 2.1 rebuilds that alphabet as a variable poster grotesk with a cargo licence: 100 to 900 in weight, 50 to 150 in width, counters drawn to hold their shape even when a letter is crushed to half its width.

Use it enormous. It gets nervous below 24 px.

Specimen story

One serif, from footnote to façade.

Falter is our scalpel: a high-contrast serif whose optical-size axis runs from 5 to 1200 — the widest we have ever cut. At opsz 5 it is a stubby, durable footnote face that survives bad paper. At 1200 the hairlines thin to razor width and the ball terminals float free of their stems. Ilya drew the two extremes eighteen months apart and refused to interpolate until every intermediate e passed the squint test.

Version 1.4 adds small caps, discretionary ligatures and oldstyle figures — the full editorial toolkit.

Specimen story

Handwriting on one end. A machine on the other.

Betrieb is the face our studio runs on: file names, invoices, terminal sessions, the label maker. Five axes take it from a brush-casual sans to a strict linear monospace — MONO for the grid, CASL for the temperature, plus weight, slant and a true cursive switch. It began as Mara’s handwriting, digitised on a delayed flight to Zürich, and hardened release by release into an instrument.

Version 3.0 ships 1,238 glyphs, twelve stylistic sets and code ligatures that know when to stay out of the way.

Colophon

Glyphaus is a two-person type foundry in Berlin-Kreuzberg, founded in 2019 by Mara Jost and Ilya Brandt. We publish at most two releases a year — slowly, loudly. Every face ships variable-first; if a glyph can’t survive being stretched from poster to caption, it doesn’t ship.

Kolosse, Falter and Betrieb are built on open-source designs — Anybody by Tyler Finck (ETC), Playfair by Claus Eggers Sørensen, Recursive by Stephen Nixon (Arrow Type) — under the SIL Open Font License. This site is a working specimen, set entirely in the faces it sells. No photographs were harmed: every image on this page is a letterform.
