The dispensary shelves run four deep and one hundred and forty-one bottles long. Ligatorium

The Bindery

A binding is a promise that a text will outlive its reader. We make the promise in linen, oak, and gold, and we have kept it, so far, for two hundred and thirty-four years. Every book passes through the same four rites, in the same order, because the order is the point.

  1. I

    Collatio

    Folios counted thrice: once by daylight, once by candlelight, once by someone who has not been told what the book is.

  2. II

    Consutura

    Sewn on linen cords in a herringbone stitch, waxed with our own bees’ wax. A book should open flat and close without being asked.

  3. III

    Corpus

    Boards of quarter-sawn oak; the spine rounded by hand with a hammer older than the shop. The book acquires a back, and opinions.

  4. IV

    Ornatus

    Gold leaf laid at dawn, when the air argues less.* Titles in 14-carat gold; sigils by arrangement, and only on the inside.

*The leaf is beaten to a ten-thousandth of a millimetre. It rides a draught the way rumour rides a parish.
