The studio rule 折り方

Three lines we never cross

One sheet.

Every model in this studio begins as a single square of Kurotani washi. Nothing is added and nothing is taken away — the bird was in the sheet all along.

No cuts.

A scissor line is a shortcut your hands never learn from. Our patterns fold clean from corner to corner, and the paper keeps its whole memory.

No glue.

Tension holds the crane together — the same tension that holds your attention for the forty quiet minutes it takes to fold one.

The paper 和紙

The paper is half the model

Our sheets come from the Yamamoto family workshop in Kurotani, a village that has made paper for eight hundred years. Long-fibre kozo, beaten by hand, dyed in small vats.
