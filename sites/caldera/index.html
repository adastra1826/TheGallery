Third High-Desert Land Art Biennale

CALDERA

Five monuments on the floor of Frayle Caldera, Owyhee Plateau. One walking route, timed to the sun. 21 June – 23 September 2027.

Scroll — the site keeps solar time.

The premise

A room with no roof, eleven kilometers wide.

Frayle Caldera is a basalt bowl on the Owyhee Plateau — eleven kilometers across, four hundred meters deep, one hour of gravel past the last paved road. Every two years the Trust commissions five permanent works for its floor. There is no gate, no ticket, and no shade.

You walk the route the sun walks: east rim to playa, 7.5 kilometers, five stations. The works are built to be read slowly — at first light, at blazing noon, at dusk, and again under a sky dark enough to see your shadow by starlight. Heat, distance, patience. These are the materials.

Orientation

Site plan

The route is one-way, westbound. Allow five hours walking — longer if you stop, and you will stop. Last water is the trailhead cistern.

Station 1 · 1.4 km from trailhead · best light 09:35

Basalt Choir

Tsering Lhamo — b. 1981, Lhasa · works in Marfa, Texas

Materials
Quarried columnar basalt, unpolished
Dimensions
33 columns, 2.1–7.4 m · field 60 × 18 m
Coordinates
42°17′08″ N · 116°42′51″ W
Catalog
CAL·27·01

Thirty-three columns stand in the phrasing of a chant — long interval, short interval, rest. In the morning their shadows lie down together and point west, deeper into the route, as if the stone already knew where you were going.

1.7 km on · cairns every 200 m · no shade

Station 2 · 3.1 km · best light 12:00

Mirror Fallow

Materials

CAL·27·02

A fallow field replanted with 217 ground-set mirrors, each pitched two degrees toward the pole star. They grow nothing but sky. At solar noon the field runs white, and the horizon above it starts to swim.

1.6 km on · the dome reads as a hill until it doesn't

Fourteen lifts of rammed earth over 1,204 accessions of native seed — bluebunch, biscuit-root, big sagebrush. The door opens one day a year, on the autumn equinox, and the seeds leave by hand.

1.5 km on · when the wind is up you will hear station 4 first

Station 4 · 6.2 km · best light 17:30

Wind Organ

Levent Aksoy — b. 1968, İzmir

Materials

Dimensions

Coordinates

Nineteen weathering-steel pipes tuned to the caldera's evening westerly. Below twelve knots it says nothing at all. Then the rim cools, the air falls downslope, and the organ holds a low D for as long as the light lasts.

Before you walk

Field guide

Water

Carry four liters per person. There is no potable water on the caldera floor; the nearest tap is the trailhead cistern — 7.5 km behind you once you reach the spiral.

Heat

The route closes at a shade temperature of 41 °C — check the flag at the trailhead kiosk. In July and August, walk before 10:00 or after 17:00.

Shuttles

Out from Bruneau Junction05:10 · 08:40 · 16:20

Return from trailhead14:00 · 21:30 · 00:40 (star shuttle)

No private vehicles past the cattle grid.

Dark sky

Bortle class 1. Astronomical dark 22:41–03:55 in midsummer. Red-filter headlamps only past station 4; the spiral viewing circle holds forty.

Pack

Broad hat, salt tablets, red-filter lamp, one warm layer — the floor loses 25 °C between noon and midnight.

Permits

Day walking is free. Overnight star camps require permit OLB-27-114-C, issued at the kiosk, twelve per night.

22:40 · night

The biennale does not close.

Moonlight is written into every commission. Lhamo's columns go silver on their east faces, and the intervals between them read like a second, slower chant. The mirror field fills with stars — 217 rectangles of them, lying in the dirt like something spilled. The dome holds the day's heat until midnight; put a palm on it. And the spiral, re-raked at each new moon, is bright enough to walk by starlight alone.
