The phenomenon · λ ≈ 525 nm

Some forests keep a lamp lit.

On a moonless night in the right wet wood, the ground answers the dark. It is not phosphorescence and not decay's afterglow — it is chemistry, run continuously by a living network. A fungal luciferin, 3‑hydroxyhispidin, is oxidised by luciferase and drops back through the caffeic‑acid cycle, releasing a photon near 525 nm: the green a human eye is most awake to in the dark.

We think it is an invitation. The glow peaks after midnight, tracks a circadian clock the mushroom keeps without a sky, and appears to court the insects that carry spores. For eleven seasons we have gone out with cameras that count single photons to learn what the fungus is saying — and to whom.

Field log · seasons 9–11

Where the lamps were lit.

Selected entries. Coordinates are logged to the site marker, not the specimen — the fungi keep their own address.

  1. 2025 · 10 · 03 35.4728° N  138.6236° E

    Aokigahara transect — Honshu

    Mycena chlorophos fruited in a flush after 61 mm of rain. Peak glow at 02:40; luminance held for 3.1 h before the caps collapsed at first grey light.

    obs. I.F. · K.N.
  2. 2025 · 08 · 19 35.5951° N  83.5075° W

    Cold Lamp Ridge — Southern Appalachians

    Panellus stipticus colonising a single fallen oak along forty metres of trunk. Foxfire visible to the unaided eye at 1.2 m once night sight had settled — no camera needed.

    obs. R.O.
  3. 2024 · 11 · 27 24.4990° S  48.4207° W

    Alto do Ribeira — Ribeira Valley, Brazil

    Confirmed the circadian dimming under continuous count: glow fell 74% between 04:00 and dawn, then recovered to full by 21:00 with no change in temperature or moisture. The clock, not the weather.

    obs. I.F. · D.M.
  4. 2024 · 06 · 14 46.9013° N  123.8021° W

    Home wood — Loam Field Station, Cascadia

    Armillaria mellea rhizomorphs lit a buried root lattice under the trail. Below the unaided threshold; recovered only in a 900-second exposure — a map of the dead roots, drawn in light.

    obs. K.N.
  5. 2023 · 09 · 02 43.6640° N  142.8544° E

    Daisetsuzan understory — Hokkaido

    Gill glow of an Omphalotus-type at 0.55 mcd/m² — the brightest single reading on record until the Ribeira season. We sat with it for four hours and said very little.

    obs. R.O. · D.M.
