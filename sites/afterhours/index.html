18th & Vine · Kansas City, Missouri · Est. 1962

After Hours

◆

A basement room, forty-one steps off Vine Street, where the ceiling is low, the lights are lower, and the music is taken seriously.

Doors at eight. First set at nine. No cover if you arrive carrying an instrument you can actually play. The coffee is black, the whiskey is honest, and in sixty-four years nobody has successfully requested “Happy Birthday.”

◆Tonight’s Sets◆

Cut live to the house system · notes by the doorman

Side A · First Set 9:00

The Ruby Calloway Trio

Ruby Calloway, voice & piano · Dexter “Dex” Whitfield, upright bass · Wm. “Papers” Monroe, drums & brushes

Ruby opens quiet on purpose. She takes “Detour Ahead” at a crawl, hands the bridge to Whitfield alone, and by the third tune the room has forgotten it ordered drinks. One Monk number played completely straight, which is somehow stranger.

Side B · Second Set 10:30

The Vine Street Five

Cornelius “Corny” Dade, cornet · Little Willie Parr, tenor · Eustace Brown, trombone · Alma Reed, piano · Sonny Okafor, drums

The Five play hot: the old 18th Street riff book at brand-new tempos. Dade quotes “Moten Swing” twice a night and denies it both times. Alma Reed’s left hand is the loudest thing in the building, and the building includes a trombone.

Dead Wax · The Late Hang 12:00

The Midnight Jam

The stand is open — house rhythm section stays up.

Bring your horn and know your keys. Don’t call “Cherokee” unless you mean it, and don’t mean it unless you’ve slept. Fastest tempos of the night, settled the traditional way: whoever blinks first counts it off.

◆The House Asks◆

Six requests, posted since opening night

  • Listen more than you talk.
  • Applaud the solo when it lands, not when it gets loud.
  • Requests go in the tip jar, folded twice.
  • Take the third step slow. Everyone misses the third step.
  • When Ruby sings “’Round Midnight,” the shakers rest.
  • Last call is 1:30. The music decides when it’s over.
