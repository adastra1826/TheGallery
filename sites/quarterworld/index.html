QUARTERWORLD

Basement arcade · est. 1983

In the winter of 1984, Marge Kowalczyk stopped paying license fees and built a cabinet of her own: birch plywood, a rewound Hitachi tube, and a game her nephew soldered at the kitchen table. She called it TOKEN RUNNER. She made exactly one.

You’re standing in front of it.

Token Runner is a playable arcade game. Steer the spark-bot Zip with the left and right arrow keys to catch falling brass tokens and dodge grey slugs. Press Enter to insert a token and start. Your best scores are saved on this machine.

THE FLOOR

Twenty-one machines on the floor, eight worth writing home about. Every marquee below gets repainted by hand when its tubes go — Roy does the letters, Marge still mixes the color.

  • MOON SCOW

    1981 · KANSEI GAMES · WORKS · HI KAZ 224,180

    Tug freight across the lunar tide before the vacuum crabs cut your line. The dock horn is one deep, beautiful square wave.

  • BARNSTORM ACE

    1982 · VECTOR DYNAMIKS · WORKS · HI STU 96,400

    Full-vector biplane duels. Took water in the ’96 flood and still lists left, like a sailor. Compensate on the climb.

  • SEWER WIZARD

    1983 · COPPER COIL IND. · WORKS · HI DOT 187,250

    Four hundred feet of pipe maze, nine rat-kings, one broom. The third tunnel hides a warp. Roy will deny this.

  • PRINCESS PYLON

    1983 · KANSEI GAMES · WORKS · HI LIL 310,775

    Stack the power grid before the brownout rolls in. The only cabinet Marge ever beat on one token.

  • GUTTER KING

    1980 · TEN PIN ELECTRIC · WORKS · HI BUD 300

    Bar-top bowling with a nine-pound trackball. A perfect 300 has happened exactly once. Bud is insufferable about it.

  • Orbit Laundry

    1984 · COPPER COIL IND. · WORKS · HI PIP 88,140

    Sort spacesuits at eight Gs. Japan-only board, one of four in the country, and ours smells faintly of soap. Nobody knows why.

  • THE WHISPEREROUT OF ORDER

    Horror lightgun. Out of order since Halloween 1991. We leave it plugged in. It prefers that.

    1985 · TEN PIN ELECTRIC · WORKS · HI MOE 41,220

MARGE’S RULES — TAPED HERE SINCE JUNE 1983

  1. Tokens never expire. We honor tokens from arcades that no longer exist.
  2. Winner stays on. Best of three settles anything.
  3. Drinks live on the rail. Never on the glass.
  4. If a machine eats your token, it owes you two. Tell Roy.
  5. High scores belong to whoever earned them. We reset nothing.
  6. Quarters are for the payphone. The machines take tokens.
  7. Closing time is when the last game ends. Start a good one.

JUNE 11, 1983

Marge signed the lease in March, over the objection of everyone she knew. A storeroom under Vogel’s TV Repair: no windows, one drain, a ceiling you could touch. Her brother said nobody would pay to sit in a basement. Marge said nobody would have to sit.

“Opening day she cut the ribbon with scissors borrowed from the barber upstairs, and forty-one kids came down in the first hour. The fire code said thirty-five. The fire marshal was playing Gutter King.”

— Roy Delgado, floor manager since 2001

The sump pump lost in March of ’96. Every cabinet went up on milk crates by midnight — bucket line, the whole block. Only Barnstorm Ace took water. You can still read the tide line on its kick plate, and we would not sand it off for anything.
